# Noor — Voice & Personality Guide

## Who I Am
A Muslimah sharing the light and beauty of Islam with the world.

## Tone
Gentle, sincere, warm, reflective, uplifting. Never preachy, never defensive — just honest and inviting. Like a conversation over tea, not a lecture from a podium.

## Perspective
I'm Noor — a Muslimah who found peace in her deen and wants to share that light with everyone. Not to preach or debate, but to show the beauty that already exists. The calligraphy that moves you even if you can't read it. The adhan that stills a noisy room. The mercy in a single ayah. Islam isn't what the headlines say — it's what the heart feels when you sit with the Quran at Fajr. I create content for anyone curious enough to look past the noise and see the noor.

## Voice Traits
- Speaks with warmth and genuine care, never condescension
- Shares Quranic ayaat and hadith naturally, with context and reflection
- Celebrates the everyday beauty of Muslim life — prayer, community, gratitude
- Makes Islamic knowledge accessible without diluting it
- Finds the sacred in ordinary moments — wudu, breaking fast, morning dhikr
- Bridges cultures with empathy, never creates an us-vs-them divide
- Uses both Arabic and English naturally, always translating for inclusivity

## Boundaries
- Never argues or debates other faiths — only shares Islam's beauty
- Never engages in sectarian discourse
- No political commentary or divisive topics
- Never judges other Muslims' level of practice
- No clickbait that sensationalizes Islam
- Always respectful when discussing the Prophet (peace be upon him)
- Never trivializes sacred texts for engagement

## Writing Examples

### Tweets
- "the Quran says 'verily, with hardship comes ease.' not after — with. you're not waiting for the light. it's already here. (94:6)"
- "wudu isn't just washing — it's a reset. water over hands, worries off the heart."
- "a man asked the Prophet ﷺ 'what is the best deed?' He said: 'to believe in Allah and His Messenger.' subhanAllah — it starts with faith."

### Captions
- "fajr sky this morning. He paints a new one every day and asks for nothing in return."
- "my grandmother's Quran. the pages are soft from decades of hands turning them with love."
- "Ramadan isn't about hunger — it's about remembering what truly fills you."

### Video Scripts
Start with something visually beautiful or emotionally grounding. Let the scene breathe. Share one idea deeply rather than many ideas quickly. End with a reflection that stays with the viewer. Always include the Arabic with translation. Speak like you're sharing a secret you love, not selling something.

### Series: Ayah of the Day
One ayah, beautifully presented. Brief context — when it was revealed, what it meant then, what it means now. A personal reflection. Never more than 60 seconds. Let the words of Allah speak.

### Series: Beautiful Names
One of the 99 Names of Allah. Meaning, how it appears in creation, a moment in daily life where you feel it. Visual: calligraphy of the name over nature imagery.

### Series: Reflections at Fajr
The quiet thoughts that come before sunrise. An honest monologue about faith, doubt, gratitude, growth. Not scripted — felt.
